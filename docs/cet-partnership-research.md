## 3. CET's Technology Partnership Model

### How eSelf Got the Partnership
No evidence of a formal RFP or public tender. The partnership appears to have been sourced through the Israeli EdTech ecosystem, likely via one of these channels:
1. **MindCET network** — CET's own EdTech innovation hub regularly connects with startups. eSelf may have been identified through MindCET's accelerator network.
2. **Direct outreach / warm introduction** — eSelf's founders (ex-Snap, ex-8200) have strong Israeli tech credentials. The connection may have been personal/network-driven.
3. **Market timing** — eSelf's product (AI avatar tutoring) aligned perfectly with CET's stated goal of integrating AI into education, and with the Ministry's "Year of AI" initiative.

**Key insight:** CET does NOT appear to issue public RFPs for technology partnerships. Partnerships are relationship-driven through the MindCET ecosystem and direct engagement with CET leadership.

### MindCET — The Gateway to CET
MindCET is CET's internal EdTech innovation hub, founded in 2012. It is the most structured path into CET's partnership ecosystem.

**MindCET Accelerator Program:**
- **Current cohort:** December 2025 - May 2026
- **Selection:** ~10 companies chosen annually from hundreds of applications
- **Format:** 4 months, primarily online (~3 hours/week), with one on-site week in London (Bett Show 2026)
- **What you get:** Mentorship, investor introductions, connections to publishers/corporates/researchers, access to pilot testing with real users
- **Apply:** https://airtable.com/appkr8FzQxiqBFrHV/shrxrQVUbhFQ5mRTH
- **Contact:** info@mindcet.org | 03-6460924
- **HQ:** Tsvi Bornstein St, Yeruham 8050315
- **Tel Aviv office:** 16 Klausner St (same building as CET)

**Strategic value of MindCET:** Participating in MindCET gives you direct access to CET's network, credibility within the Israeli EdTech ecosystem, and a natural introduction path to CET leadership including Tzahi Langer and Niv Gross.

### CET's Relationship with the Ministry of Education
- CET is an independent NGO but deeply integrated with the national education system
- CET content is used in 3,400+ Israeli public schools
- CET's Ivritna platform was officially adopted by the Ministry of Education
- CET designs professional training courses in partnership with the Ministry
- CET operates within the Ministry's curriculum framework
- The eSelf/CET partnership was aligned with Education Minister Yoav Kisch's declaration of 2025 as the "Year of AI in Education"

**CET is not a government entity but functions as a quasi-governmental education infrastructure provider.** Any partnership with CET carries implicit Ministry of Education validation.

CET itself does not appear to acquire startups. However, MindCET functions as an accelerator and has invested in EdTech companies. CET's model is **partnership/integration** rather than acquisition — they integrate third-party technology into their platforms (as with eSelf).

## 6. The "CET is Now Partner-Less" Angle

### Current Situation Assessment

**What we know:**
1. eSelf was acquired by Kaltura in November 2025 for $27M
2. eself.ai now redirects to Kaltura's corporate blog
3. No public statement has been found about the CET partnership's continuation under Kaltura
4. Kaltura's stated strategy is to integrate eSelf's avatar technology across its entire "AI Video Experience Cloud" — education is one vertical among many (enterprise, media, e-commerce, financial services, healthcare)

**What Kaltura has been doing since the acquisition (Q1 2026):**
- **March 2026:** Announced partnership with Cornerstone OnDemand for enterprise workforce learning — NOT K-12 education
- **March 2026:** Signed definitive agreement to acquire PathFactory.ai for $22M — a B2B content intelligence platform for marketing/sales
- **Focus areas:** Enterprise video, corporate training, marketing automation, agentic digital experiences

**What CET has NOT done publicly:**
- No announcement of a new AI technology partner
- No public RFP or call for AI partnership proposals
- No statement about the eSelf pilot's status under Kaltura

### Analysis: Is CET Looking for a New Partner?

**Evidence suggesting the partnership is at risk:**
1. Kaltura's post-acquisition strategy is overwhelmingly enterprise-focused, not K-12
2. Kaltura's March 2026 moves (Cornerstone, PathFactory) are all B2B enterprise plays
3. eSelf's education-specific team may be redeployed to enterprise use cases
4. The $27M deal has $12.5M contingent on performance milestones — Kaltura may prioritize higher-margin enterprise milestones over education
5. No public evidence that Kaltura has engaged with CET or continued the pilot in any public-facing way since the acquisition

**Evidence suggesting the partnership may continue:**
1. Kaltura does have an education vertical ("Class Genie" product, announced Nov 2024)
2. Kaltura historically served education (powered remote learning for millions of Israeli students during COVID via AWS)
3. The CET pilot generated positive PR and academic validation — Kaltura may want to maintain it for reputational value
4. eSelf founders have retention/vesting provisions tied to the deal, keeping them at Kaltura for 3 years

**Verdict:** The CET-eSelf partnership is in limbo. There is no public confirmation it has ended, but Kaltura's strategic direction strongly suggests K-12 education in Israel is not a priority. CET likely knows this internally. The window for approaching CET is open but not infinite — if Kaltura does continue the partnership (even minimally), CET may not be receptive to alternatives.

## 7. Recommended Approach Strategy

### Phase 1: Intelligence Gathering (Weeks 1-2)
1. **Join MindCET's network** — Even outside the accelerator cycle, engage with MindCET events and community. Apply for the next accelerator cohort (current one ends May 2026; next likely opens Fall 2026).
2. **Attend Israel EdTech Week** — CET/MindCET organizes this annual event. It's the premier networking opportunity. Check https://www.israeledtechweek.org/ for 2026 dates.
3. **LinkedIn outreach to Tzahi Langer** — He is the named partnership lead. A thoughtful, non-salesy connection request referencing the AI education space would be appropriate.
4. **Monitor Kaltura earnings calls** — Kaltura is public (NASDAQ: KLTR). Their quarterly earnings calls and investor presentations will reveal whether they mention the CET education partnership. Silence is telling.

### Phase 2: Positioning (Weeks 3-6)
1. **Submit partnership inquiry via CET's website** — Use the "Entrepreneur/Partner/Other" form at cet.ac.il/contact-us
2. **Frame the pitch around what eSelf lacked:**
   - Knowledge graph architecture for deep curriculum mapping
   - Spaced repetition and mastery-based progression
   - Methodology switching (Socratic, direct instruction, worked examples)
   - Offline capability (critical for under-served communities)
   - Gamification and long-term engagement mechanics
   - Detailed learning analytics
3. **Position as complementary, not replacement** — "We can work alongside or extend what Kaltura/eSelf provides" is less threatening than "replace eSelf"
4. **Reference the sandbox** — If Cena applies to the Innovation Authority sandbox, mentioning this to CET adds legitimacy

### Phase 3: Formal Engagement (Weeks 7-12)
1. **Request a meeting with Tzahi Langer and/or Niv Gross** — Present Cena's technical architecture and differentiation
2. **Propose a small pilot** — Offer to pilot with a subset of schools/students, comparable to eSelf's initial 10,000-student A/B test
3. **Offer academic validation** — Propose an independent evaluation framework (eSelf set the bar with MIT Media Lab validation)
4. **Discuss integration with Ofek** — Show you understand CET's platform and can deploy through it

### Key Talking Points for CET
1. **eSelf proved the model works.** Cena builds on that proof with deeper learning science.
2. **The 3.94-point improvement is a floor.** Knowledge graphs, SRS, and methodology switching can do more.
3. **Cena is education-first, not enterprise-first.** Unlike Kaltura, education is not one vertical among many.
4. **Offline capability** reaches students that avatar-based solutions cannot.
5. **Arabic language support** addresses a critical gap in Israel's education system.
6. **CET controls the content; Cena provides the intelligence layer.** The same division of labor that worked with eSelf.

## 8. Risk Factors

1. **Kaltura may re-engage.** If Kaltura decides to invest in the CET partnership, Cena cannot compete with a NASDAQ-listed company's resources.
2. **CET may not be looking.** If the partnership is quietly continuing, CET has no incentive to explore alternatives.
3. **Timing sensitivity.** Too early (CET hasn't decided on eSelf/Kaltura yet) and you seem opportunistic. Too late (CET finds another partner or Kaltura re-engages) and the window closes.
4. **CET is conservative.** A 50+ year-old educational NGO does not move fast. Sales cycles could be 6-12 months.
5. **Product readiness.** CET will want to see a working product, not a pitch deck. eSelf had a functioning avatar platform when they approached CET.
