# Cena Privacy Policy

**Status: DRAFT — awaiting Cena legal counsel review**
**Last updated: 2026-04-11**

## 1. Introduction

Cena ("we", "us", "our") operates the Cena adaptive learning platform (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have regarding your data. This policy applies to all users of the Service, including students, parents, guardians, teachers, and administrators.

## 2. Data We Collect

We collect the minimum data necessary to provide and improve the Service:

- **Email address** — used for account creation, authentication, and account recovery.
- **Display name** — chosen by the user, shown to classmates and teachers.
- **Date of birth** — used for age verification and consent tier classification. Never displayed to other users.
- **Learning activity data** — session duration, questions answered, accuracy, mastery levels, and progress metrics.
- **AI tutor conversations** — messages exchanged with the AI tutor, used to provide personalized educational guidance.
- **Device and usage data** — browser type, operating system, IP address (anonymized), and interaction patterns for service reliability and improvement.

## 3. Purpose of Collection

We use your data for the following purposes:

- **Adaptive learning** — to personalize question difficulty, pacing, and content to each learner's level.
- **Authentication** — to verify your identity and protect your account.
- **Progress tracking** — to show you, your teachers, and (for minors) your parents how your learning is progressing.
- **Safety and compliance** — to enforce age-appropriate content policies and comply with applicable laws.
- **Service improvement** — to analyze aggregated, anonymized usage patterns to improve the platform.

## 4. Legal Basis for Processing

- **Consent** (GDPR Art 6(1)(a)) — for users aged 16 and above who create an account.
- **Parental consent** (GDPR Art 8, COPPA 16 CFR §312.5) — for users under 16. We collect verifiable parental consent before processing any child's personal data.
- **Contract performance** (GDPR Art 6(1)(b)) — processing necessary to provide the Service you registered for.
- **Legitimate interest** (GDPR Art 6(1)(f)) — for anonymized analytics and platform security. We balance our interests against your rights and freedoms.

## 5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described above:

- **Account data** (email, name) — retained while the account is active, deleted within 30 days of account deletion request.
- **Learning data** (sessions, progress) — retained while the account is active. Anonymized aggregates may be retained for research after account deletion.
- **AI tutor conversations** — retained for 12 months from creation, then automatically purged.
- **Date of birth** — retained in the consent audit trail for the lifetime of the account plus 3 years (legal compliance).
- **Device/usage data** — IP addresses anonymized within 90 days. Aggregated metrics retained indefinitely.

## 6. Third-Party Data Processors

We share personal data with the following processors, each bound by data processing agreements:

- **Anthropic** (San Francisco, USA) — AI tutor service. Processes conversation messages to generate educational responses. Subject to Anthropic's data processing addendum. Conversations are not used to train Anthropic's models.
- **Google Firebase** (Google Cloud, various regions) — authentication services, database hosting, and analytics. Subject to Google Cloud's data processing terms.

## 7. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your jurisdiction, including the United States. We ensure adequate protection through:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission.
- Adequacy decisions where applicable.
- Supplementary measures as required by Schrems II guidance.

For Israeli users: transfers comply with the Israel Protection of Privacy Regulations (Transfer of Data to Databases Abroad), 5761-2001.

## 8. Your Rights

Under GDPR, Israel PPL, and applicable law, you have the right to:

- **Access** — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
- **Rectification** — request correction of inaccurate personal data.
- **Erasure** ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations.
- **Data portability** — receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Withdraw consent** — withdraw your consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
- **Object** — object to processing based on legitimate interest.
- **Lodge a complaint** — file a complaint with your local data protection authority (e.g., the Israeli Privacy Protection Authority, a EU supervisory authority, or the US FTC for COPPA matters).

## 9. Data Subject Access Requests (DSAR)

To exercise any of your rights, email privacy@cena.edu with the subject line "DSAR". We will verify your identity and respond within 30 calendar days. If we need more time (up to an additional 60 days for complex requests), we will notify you within the initial 30-day window.

## 10. Parent and Guardian Rights

Parents and guardians of minor users have additional rights under COPPA and GDPR:

- **Review** — request a copy of all personal data collected from your child.
- **Delete** — request deletion of your child's personal data and account.
- **Revoke consent** — withdraw your consent for further data collection, which will result in account deactivation.

To exercise these rights, email privacy@cena.edu from the parent email address registered during account creation.
